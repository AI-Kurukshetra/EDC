## Opening

“Clinical trials still run across disconnected spreadsheets, email threads, static PDFs, and fragmented systems. That creates delays, poor visibility, audit risk, and slower decisions.

Clinical Data Hub brings study operations into one workflow-driven platform. It connects study setup, subjects, eCRF capture, queries, documents, exports, audit trails, and electronic signatures in a single operational system.”

Open:

- `/login`

Log in.

---

## Dashboard

“Right after login, the platform gives an operational command view. Instead of hunting across tools, teams immediately see active studies, subject volume, open queries, pending work, and recent activity.”

Point to:

- active studies
- total subjects
- open queries
- unread tasks
- recent activity

“Every role gets a live view of execution, not just static reporting.”

---

## Account and Study Entry

Open:

- `/account`

“Each user also has a role-aware account workspace with assignments, responsibilities, and notifications.”

Then open:

- `/studies`

Open the seeded study.

---

## Study Shell

“Inside a study, the platform becomes the operating system for execution. Everything is organized in one shell: forms, subjects, data capture, queries, documents, exports, and audit.”

On the overview page:

“Leadership gets study readiness, enrollment progress, and study-level sign-off in one place. That means operational oversight is built into the workflow, not bolted on afterward.”

---

## Forms

Open:

- `/studies/[studyId]/forms`

“Protocol execution depends on controlled forms. Here, CRFs are versioned and published templates are locked from direct editing. Any change goes through a next-version workflow, which protects approved history and reduces downstream risk.”

Show:

- published form
- locked state
- next version path

---

## Subjects

Open:

- `/studies/[studyId]/subjects`

“Subject management is no longer external to the system. Teams can enroll participants directly into the study, track lifecycle state, and maintain operational context at the roster level.”

Show:

- enroll subject form
- roster cards
- lifecycle update controls
- withdrawal capture

“This turns the subject roster from a passive list into an operational workflow surface.”

---

## Data Capture

Open:

- `/studies/[studyId]/data`

“This is the live eCRF workspace. Teams can select a subject, pick a published CRF, capture visit-based responses, save drafts, and submit entries for review.”

Show:

- subject selection
- CRF selection
- visit handling
- entry form

Then point to sign-off:

“Once data is submitted, it can be electronically signed and locked. That gives the platform a stronger compliance and accountability model around study data, with clear certification meaning and immutable audit traceability.”

---

## Queries

Open:

- `/studies/[studyId]/queries`

“Most systems treat data queries as disconnected follow-up. Here, query management is native to the study workflow.”

Show:

- response history
- assignment controls
- status controls
- response textarea

“Teams can triage, respond, reassign, and close the loop in one place. That shortens resolution cycles and improves study visibility.”

---

## Documents

Open:

- `/studies/[studyId]/documents`

“Document governance is also embedded in the product. Teams can register documents, maintain version lineage, capture electronic signatures, and prevent in-place mutation of approved records.”

Show:

- document register
- version history
- signature state
- next version workflow

“That means the system preserves both speed and control.”

---

## Exports

Open:

- `/studies/[studyId]/export`

“Exports are not just file downloads. They are controlled operational events.”

Show:

- export format
- signature meaning
- password re-entry
- export history

“Every export request is signed, time-stamped, and tracked. That creates much stronger accountability around data movement.”

---

## Audit

Open:

- `/studies/[studyId]/audit`

“And because all of this happens in one system, the audit trail becomes meaningful. Subject actions, CRF submissions, query responses, document events, signatures, and exports are all traceable as part of the same study record.”

---

## Closing

“Clinical Data Hub is not just a UI for study records. It is an execution layer for clinical operations.

It helps teams move from fragmented coordination to controlled, traceable workflows across the full study lifecycle.

That means better visibility, faster execution, stronger governance, and a more scalable operating model for modern clinical studies.”
